Frequently Asked Questions

Understand the FAA HIMS process, pilot concerns, and reform efforts based on real community feedback.

What is the FAA HIMS Program?

HIMS (Human Intervention Motivation Study) is an FAA program originally designed for pilots recovering from substance abuse. Over time, it has expanded to cover a range of mental health conditions, often with the same protocols—lengthy monitoring, expensive evaluations, and stigma.

What are pilots saying in forums?

In communities like r/flying and the HIMS Victims forum, pilots have voiced concern about the program’s broad scope:

“It’s no longer just drug/alcohol—it’s mental health, ADHD, PTSD, depression—they’re all treated the same with excessive testing and stigma.”
“The system needs science and compassion, not punishment. We’re pilots, not criminals.”

This reflects a growing sentiment that reform is not optional—it’s urgent.

What organizations are leading reform?

  • Pilots for HIMS Reform: A grassroots group pushing for legislative change including the Pilots for HIMS Reform Act of 2025.
  • FAAHIMSProgram.com: An independent site advocating for transparency, oversight, and peer support in the HIMS space.

How do I connect with others in the same situation?

The best place to start is the HIMS Victims Forum. Many pilots have shared detailed timelines, medical experiences, legal resources, and encouragement. You are not alone.

Is there a petition or campaign?

Yes. Pilots are urging the FAA and Congress to consider reforms that protect both air safety and individual dignity. Look for discussions and shared petitions on Reddit and the HIMS Victims Forum.
