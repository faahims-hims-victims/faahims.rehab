FAA HIMS FAQ & Reform Resources

What is the FAA HIMS Program?

The Human Intervention Motivation Study (HIMS) program is the FAA’s path for pilots needing medical reinstatement due to substance-use or mental health conditions. Critics highlight concerns such as opaque decision-making, one-size-fits-all procedures, and potential for unfair flagging.

Where can I learn more or get involved?

  • Pilots for HIMS Reform: A grassroots group advocating for transparency and legislative reform, including the proposed Pilots for HIMS Reform Act of 2025. They offer tools, personal stories, and legal insights. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • FAAHIMSProgram.com: Independent site discussing the need for oversight, scientific standards, and individualized support within HIMS. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

What are pilots in forums saying?

On pilot forums and Reddit communities like r/flying, sentiment is high. One pilot sums it up:

“It’s no longer just drug/alcohol—it’s mental health, ADHD, PTSD, depression—they’re all treated the same with excessive testing and stigma.” :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Others emphasize that reform is critical, not optional: they advocate for scientific, empathetic approaches that let pilots return to flying with dignity. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
