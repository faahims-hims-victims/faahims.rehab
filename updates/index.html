FAA HIMS Reform Updates

Stay informed on legislation, grassroots advocacy, and personal stories shaping the future of FAA’s HIMS program.

  • Legislation Update: Pilots Push for Reform Bill

    The Pilots for HIMS Reform advocacy group reports that the “Pilots for HIMS Reform Act of 2025” is being prepared for Congressional introduction—seeking transparency and medically informed FAA policy.

  • Policy Update: Scientific Standards for HIMS

    Analysis from Pilots for HIMS Reform and reform blogs highlights how the HIMS process has expanded beyond substance use, now encompassing mental health conditions like ADHD and PTSD with little differentiation, creating undue burden and stigma. “While it began as drug/alcohol, it no longer is JUST drug/alcohol. ... all of these cases go through the HIMS process.” —Reddit pilot testimony

  • Voices from the Flight Deck

    On Reddit’s r/flying: “Regardless of what got you into the HIMS program ... you will be treated with the same social stigmas and unfairness.” This sentiment reflects widespread concern among pilots over one-size-fits-all oversight protocols.

  • Court Ruling: FAA Overturns Denial on Antidepressant Use

    A federal appeals court ruled that FAA’s denial of a medical certificate for antidepressant use was “arbitrary and capricious,” opening doors to medical precedent and potentially reshaping HIMS protocols. —Source: AOPA report
